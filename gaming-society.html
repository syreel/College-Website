A study was done on the use of active video games such as dance games and others. This study included testing of using these types of games in pe class in schools vs traditional school pe class methods.

The typical view of gaming is that children who game are quite obese and get no exercise but this quote sums up this quite well:

“But if a kid hates playing dodge ball but loves Dance Dance Revolution why not let him work up a sweat playing E-games?”

It states that not all children or teenagers are into the outdoor activities or other sporty activities therefore they won’t actually do much exercise at all. Through the use of active video games it gives the children something to do e.g. play the game instead of just “pointlessly” run around therefore it may make children more likely to do some physical activities.

Not all children are the same and what children have an interest in or like to do in their free time changes from child to child therefore if we want children to get more active we really need to start appealing to all the children rather than just the ones that are into the physical activities and other sporty stuff.

Playing an active game makes the ‘chore’ of exercise not so bad because you have something to focus your thoughts on and therefore after playing for a while you may actually start to forget that you are getting exercise while playing these type of games therefore even if it is the only form of activities that you do then it is still better than nothing.

Using this method we can use video games to do the exact opposite of making children obese because children may do more exercise than they may do otherwise.

From: http://psychcentral.com/news/2013/01/11/video-games-can-up-kids-physical-activity-reduce-obesity/50277.html

Another benefit for the positive effects on the body from games is that video games can (as shown in a study) actually improve your eyesight in certain cases.

This mostly applies to people either with caterax and similar. The study showed that for some people with caterax they could improve their vision through playing fps games including call of duty. Playing fast paced games such as these forces the player to focus on what they are doing and they require a lot of attention. Playing these fast paced games can make your body product more dopamine this makes the player happier and this will continue after the gameplay ends.

Story 3

Studies show that playing games can lead to better hand eye coordination and tracking. For example people who play FPS games have shown to be able to track things better due to the intense concentration required in order to play game. There have been studies that show that even adults can gain an effect from playing action computer games for example fighter pilots and surgeons can gain an advantage from playing computer games and they can gain better fine motor skills.

Story 5:

Research shows that people who play a lot of video games tend to have a lower performance in schools, a study showed that students that were heavily addicted to games at home tended to argue with the teachers and fight with their friends. Because of this behaviour at school it will make the student less able to learn in school and therefore they will end up with lower grades.

In this study many students that were asked told the researchers that their gaming habits were often responsible for their low grades. Although some studies have shown that video games can help students concentration this is usually in the short term and their long term concentration is negatively impacted because of this.

Story 6:

Studies show that people who play a lot of video games such as survival games or adventure games where resources are limited and scarce makes the player better at managing this in real life.  In survival games you need to preserve your food and water for example as well as any other resources you may need to keep in order surviving. The management of these recourses in these games gives you the practice in holding yourself back and saving things therefore when you go into the real world you will be more likely to save money and other resources therefore you may find dealing with money easier. This also applies to games in which you are roll playing for example where you may be doing a job and getting money then you will need to save up that money and handle it well so that you can buy the things that you want to buy in the game. These types of games give you good practice for real life where you will need to be earning money and saving money to buy things that you want.

Story 7:

Many people who play games on a regular basis make a lot more connections within their brain which is mostly a good thing and gives a positive impact on the gamer’s mental performance. Research showed that people who play lots of fast paced video games cause “certain brain networks that process vision or hearing are more likely to have enhanced connectivity to the so-called salience network.” Therefore their mental abilities are heightened. These increases in the links in the brains will likely increase the person’s ability to perceive visual stimulus as well as focus attention to a specific place more robustly.

 

These changes in the real world can enable the person to think more effectively as well as take in more information from their environment. This will be useful because for example when a person is driving it could improve their ability to perceive potential issues on the road and therefore increase their road safety.

Many puzzle games may also cause this kind of change which could lead to the person being able to do puzzles in real life such as at work it could allow the person to soceed in the workplace more because they will be better able to deal with challenges posed by their job or their boss. Being able to work more effectively will be a massive advantage in life because they will understand potential issues in their work and get around them or solve them more efficiently than they could otherwise

Story 8:

Decrease in self control

Studies have shown that highly violent video games can lead to a decrease in self-control for the players of these games. These changes are important because in everyday life you are required to exercise self-control and if you have a psychological inability to do so then you may have problems in your future life.

For example when you are trying to get a job if you have no self-control then you will be unlikely to get the job because you need to be able to deal with the situations that occur in the workplace.

The study found that children who were able to hold out on temptation and wait longer for the gratification and when they were checked up on a few years after the experiment they were “more popular with friends and family and more successful in universities and jobs.”

 

Link: https://www.psychologytoday.com/blog/get-psyched/201401/violent-video-games-decrease-self-control
